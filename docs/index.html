Data Overview

This analysis combines economic data from the World Bank with child welfare indicators from UNICEF to explore relationships between national economic conditions and child outcomes.

World Bank

  • GDP and GDP per capita
  • Public debt
  • Tax revenue
  • Government expenses
  • Inflation rates
  • Poverty headcount ratios
  • Gini index

UNICEF

  • Under-5 mortality rate
  • Infant mortality rate
  • Neonatal mortality rate
  • Stunting prevalence
  • Wasting prevalence
  • Low birth weight
  • Child poverty measures

Child Mortality Trends

Global child mortality rates have declined significantly over the past decades, but disparities remain between regions and income groups.

Nutrition Indicators

Malnutrition indicators including stunting, wasting, and underweight prevalence vary significantly across countries and are closely linked to economic conditions.

Poverty and Child Welfare

The relationship between poverty rates and child welfare outcomes demonstrates the importance of economic development in improving children's lives.

Methodology

Data Collection

Data was collected from the World Bank API and UNICEF data warehouse, covering 217 countries from 1960 to 2023.

Indicators

The dataset includes 54 indicators across economic, mortality, nutrition, and poverty categories.

Limitations

Data availability varies by country and year. Not all indicators have complete coverage across all countries and time periods.

Data sources: World Bank Open Data, UNICEF Data Warehouse

Analysis conducted using Python and visualized with Chart.js
